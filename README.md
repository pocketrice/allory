Put simply, clever and neat anecdotes centered around maximally efficient yet malleable 3D graphics integration without bloated overbearing libraries, sampling from clever and neat anecdotes from across many disciplines entrenched in the burgeoning field of computer graphics. _Making everything a single project promotes intermixing of design takeaways and motivates the creation of a unified visualization/testing toolkit!_

Optimistically, this generalizes further into the most abstract forms of computing, guided by the perception that a temporal multidisciplinary analysis may shed light on the overtly obscure concept of human-computing interfaces. 

I believe in the idea that there are better methods of interfacing between humans and computers, and the varied forms of very human interpretation, raw emotion, storytelling, and ultimately lived experience demonstrated by the few examples above prove that it's possible. Paradigm shifts necessitate acute awareness of both the knowledge graciously provided by the past as well as a humble recognition of the vastness of the future and _allory_ serves as a personal avenue for such deliberation.

A world where we engage with computers in a more ethical, conscious, liberating, and distinctly human manner means a more _human_ world!
